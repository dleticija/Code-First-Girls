Morag Coleman

Manager

Hi my name is Morag Coleman, Manager of FFSA. I have had many years' experience in the voluntary and statutory sectors supporting children and their families, as a paid and unpaid worker. I started working for Families First in September 2011. As my role involves a lot of the behind the scenes stuff, such as carrying out tasks for the directors, attracting funding and bringing everyone's ideas together, I don't get to meet many of the people we work with. However, when I do, I have a great time and really enjoy getting to know them. It also helps me to keep grounded and reminds me of the reason I do my job.

Alison Miller

Children’s Group Work Coordinator

Hi! My name is Alison Miller and I am the Children's Group Work Coordinator at Families First. I organise and run all of the group work at Families First. This includes 13+ Groups, Schools work, Transition Groups and Holiday Activities. I have a strong group of volunteers who help out with these groups who I also support individually. I got into this area of work after having volunteered at different groups and organisations such as youth groups and dance classes since 2003. I went on to study Community Education at Dundee University and from there, I joined Families First in my current position where I have been since 2010. Every day is an enjoyable one at Families First! Alison is currently on maternity leave until July 2017.

Jan Clarke

One-to-One Befriending Coordinator

Hi, my name is Jan Clarke, Befriending Coordinator for Families First. I have had many years' experience in the care and support sector working with children and adults with a variety of additional needs. I started working for Families First in May 2016 and every day brings new challenges and opportunities. I have a large and committed volunteer group who help make Families First the success it is today. The best part of my role is the variety of different people I get to meet. I’m inspired by them every single day and feel honoured to be part of their lives.

Jane Fraser

Office Manager

I arrived in the St Andrews area a few years ago and was delighted to encounter Families First which I had heard about because my daughter, who had been a student here, used to volunteer as a befriender. My background in administration both overseas and in the UK prepared me well for the position of Office Manager. I've been with the organisation since August 2011. Part of my role is to be the first point of contact when people phone or call in at the office. I take this seriously because we want to be both welcoming and helpful to the many people who we come into contact with, whether they are service users, other agencies, volunteers or members of the community. I also take care of a lot of the day to day administration, which includes processing volunteer applications. It is a joy to meet so many volunteers, most of whom are students, who are willing to give their time and energy to supporting this work.
