Volunteering can be one of the most rewarding and beneficial choices many of us will make in life. The reason that volunteers choose to volunteer can be varied, such as wanting to give something back, meeting new people, making new friends or learning new skills. Whatever your motivation, Families First is prepared to welcome you and support you. If you choose to volunteer with Families First, it not only benefits you, as you develop new skills and make a valuable contribution, it benefits the organisation and the people who use our services. With your help, we are able to deliver the services we commit to and ensure that our service users receive a quality service that meets and supports their needs.

We have a strong commitment to the volunteers who work with us. We have on average 80 volunteers who support the work we do. Families First volunteers hold a number of roles within the organisation which include Trustees, administration, driving, and direct service delivery.

One-to-One Befriending


Befrienders work with us to support children and young people on a one-to-one basis. The type of support provided depends on what has been agreed with the child, their primary carers and other agencies in terms of need and the child’s goals. This support usually takes place after school in the Rose Lane Centre. You need to have a genuine interest in promoting the children and young people’s right and well-being. We are looking for caring people who are good listeners and have interests in activities such as sports, arts and crafts, cooking, playing games, going for walks and hanging out!

Befrienders need to commit to a minimum of approximately 2 hours per fortnight for befriending sessions, plus additional time for supervision, training and briefing sessions.

Children's Group Work


Volunteers who work with children and young people in a group work setting could be involved in a number of ways ranging from youth groups, holiday activities to schools work. The purpose of our group activities is to create opportunities for children to work and socialise together in a positive way. We work with children and young people to develop their skills in team building, communication, sharing and addressing their worries in a constructive way. If you like to be part of a team and work with others, this could be a role for you.

Some of the groups only run during term time and others run during the holidays; some are weekly and some are run every fortnight. Volunteers in children's group work need to commit to a minimum of 1-2 hours per week (depending on the activity), plus additional time for supervision, training and briefing sessions.

Family Support


Family support is based on supporting the adults involved in a child’s life; again we can do this through one-to-one and group work activities. We usually support the adult to achieve the goals they have set. Our Parents and Carers group (PaCs) is an excellent platform for peer support and the sharing of ideas and experiences. We welcome enquiries from volunteers who have skills in, or would like to develop their skills in supporting adults.

Some of the support is only offered during term time and others all year round. This service also organises the family fun days and activities. Volunteers who work with adults will be expected to commit to at least 1-2 hours in their chosen activity, plus additional time for supervision, training and briefing sessions.

Driving


The children and young people we support live all over rural North East Fife. Some are very isolated because they live in rural areas. Our volunteer drivers help us to get children and young people who live in these areas to their activities with us. Drivers must have a full clean driving licence and be able to have business use included on their car insurance certificate.

All drivers will be reimbursed with their out of pocket expenses. Drivers should be confident in communicating with children who have additional support needs. We would love to hear from you if you have an hour or two to spare every week or fortnight, we mostly need drivers in the evening. You will also need to commit to additional time for supervision, training and briefing sessions.

Admin


If you are looking for a volunteering role where you want to support a charity, but are not so keen to work directly with people, this volunteering role may suit you better. We are always looking for people to support the administration functions of the organisation in roles such as collating and inputting data, preparing volunteer training packs, filing, shredding, archiving and managing resources.

The time commitment for any of these roles is negotiable to suit you and can be discussed further with our office manager. You will also need to commit to additional time for supervision, training and briefing sessions.

Your Role?


Anyone who is interested in volunteering with us should join our Facebook page or look out or further announcements on our website of when the next Volunteer Information Meeting (VIM) is. These meetings enable you to find out more about volunteering for Families First, the services we offer and whether we are the organisation you would like to volunteer with.

These meetings give participants a better understanding of our organisation and the frequency of our services as they vary greatly depending on time of year and who uses them. If you decide to continue on with us and become a volunteer you will receive full training and support to carry out your role. You will be allocated a named supervisor who will provide you with additional support should you require it. All volunteers who work directly with vulnerable adults and children will be required to complete a PVG application form.
